Day 3 came bright and cheery and warm. Not a cloud in the sky. It was to be a long, hot day. Over 80 miles to 3 Forks. We started the day by passing the state capital and couldn't resist the temptation to ham it up.

Jessica took the lead.

Enough fooling around. From there we headed out on a long, flat, valley floor.

Doug and John in front of some local mountains.

The road here was surprisingly challenging because the shoulder was fairly narrow, they had "rumble strips" preventing easy maneuvering, and we were constantly having to pass slower riders.
It was in this area that we had the first of two mishaps on the trip. We were in a pace line, and going pretty fast. We came upon a slow rider quite quickly, and unfortunately there was a car in the lane next to us, so we all slammed on the brakes. Jessica ran into my rear wheel and flipped over onto the shoulder. She got a nice road rash, and a nasty bump on the head. We convinced her to hop in the van and meet us in camp.
The rest of the day was fairly uneventful. After lunch, we got onto a long, boring, flat stretch. At about mile 65, they routed us onto a detour to a small town called 3Forks. When we got there, they had an outpost set up with kids from the town greeting us. Very cute. Here John relaxes in front of a cafe before our last dash to camp. Across the street, Brett/Doug are buying some wine/champagne to smuggle into camp for later that night.

We hammered the last 12 miles or so into camp, only to find that we were camping in a huge open area we named "The dust bowl". No trees, no grass, just some weeds. Here we enjoy our hard-won libations and thank Jessica for putting up our tents.

The view was quite enjoyable however, and the night was clear and warm.
