At day-break, we hit the "dusty" trail. By this time, we had the daily routine down pretty well. Dress, port-o-potty, breakfast, read about today's route, break down camp, check the bike, get water/gatorade, hit the road. Sleeping on the ground was becoming less painful.

We immediately rode through some gorgeous canyons and then out into some rolling farmland. Bart gave us a lecture on the benefits of the center-pivot irrigation systems. I regaled the team with stories of my summers on my uncles' farm in South Dakota. Here we stop to enjoy the local flora.

Outpost 2 was at a classic general store in some small town. Now if only the guy I handed the camera to could have framed the photo better. How hard is that? Thanks for including the yellow stripe on the road. Jeez.

We ate lunch in a small town called Twin Bridges I believe. They all blur together after a while: Tosco, Townsend, Twin Bridges...

After lunch we got into some interesting foothills. Eventually we ended up going through the old west town of Virginia City, where I got this action photo of Doug.

After Virginia City, we had a pretty serious climb, at the top of which I snapped this shot looking back down toward Virginia City.

From there, we hit the jackpot. A 7-mile long downhill that was smooth, banked, and only mildly curved. Wow. We all were doing 45-55mph. Awesome. Here we are at the bottom. If you look closely you see can see the bugs in our teeth from the smiling.

A few miles down the road, we reached the town of Ennis, and stopped for a milkshake to celebrate.

Today's camp-site was adjacent to an RV park. We set up our tents in a field containing some serious rocks, cactus, etc. The other bad news is that Doug left us today, due to a wedding commitment. He took a shuttle over to Bozeman and flew out the next day. The good news is that I had no tentmate for the rest of the week. Woohoo! Party at my place!
Tonight we did laundry, which consisted of a bucket, a hose, some soap, and a fence to dry the clothes on.
